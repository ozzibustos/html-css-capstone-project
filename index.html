Biography: 2014-Present

Dir En Grey is a Japanese heavy metal band that was formed in February 1997 and is currently signed to Firewall Div., a Free-Will sub-division. With guitarists Kaoru and Die, vocalist Kyo, drummer Shinya, and bassist Toshiya on board. They released their first EP, Missa, a few months later and gained mainstream attention in 1998, when their songs "Jealous" and "-I'll-" reached the top ten on the Oricon music charts.During the first half of 1999, X Japan co-founder Yoshiki Hayashi arranged and produced five subsequent singles. Gauze, the band's first full-length album, followed.

Vocalist Kyo was hospitalized with hearing problems in 2000, several dates of Dir En Grey's tour in support of their then-upcoming album, Macabre, had to be rescheduled. Tour 00 >> 01 Macabre eventually launched later that year. Vulgar was released in September 2003, and tours in support of the album continued into 2004.

The following year, in conjunction with the release of their third full-length album, Kiso, Dir En Grey made their first international appearances, touring China, Taiwan, and South Korea. In the same year, they released the single "Child Prey," which served as the first track on their fourth album, Vulgar. Vulgar was released in September 2003, and tours in support of the album continued into 2004.

Dir En Grey made their first appearance in Europe in 2005. Their shows in Berlin and Paris (as part of the It Withers and Withers tour) were completely sold out. Withering to Death was also the group's first official European release in 2005. Touring expanded to the United States in early 2006. The band performed at the South by Southwest festival in Austin, Texas, the Avalon Club in New York City, and the Wiltern Theatre in Los Angeles, California, before the North American release of Withering to Death.

In September, the band embarked on another international tour, this time named after their most recent single "Dozing Green," which began in Japan and ended in Europe. A second Japanese tour, titled Tour 08 The Rose Trims Again, began on September 10, the same day as their single "Glass Skin" was released. Dir En Grey announced the release of their seventh studio album, Uroboros, on August 1, 2008, which was released on November 11, 2008, in the United States. In support of the album, the band also toured the United States and Canada, with dates scheduled in November and December.

The single "Lotus" was released on January 26, 2011, along with a new fan-club-only tour titled The Decomposition of the Moon, which was set to take place from May 14 to 26. "Different Sense" was released on June 22 by the band. Dum Spiro Spero, their eighth studio album, was released on August 3. This album's material was featured on the band's Age Quod Agis South and North American tour in November and December 2011.

Arche, Dir En Grey's ninth studio album, was released on December 10, 2014. The album was accompanied by the 2014-2015 tour By the Grace of God after its release. In April and May of 2015, the tour was followed by another called The Unstoppable Life. Dir En Grey performed at Luna Sea's Lunatic Fest at Makuhari Messe on June 27, 2015. In July 2016, the band released "Utafumi," a single. From June to November 2016, they also embarked on various tours based on previous albums, including Tour16-17 From Depression To _______ [mode of Vulgar], Tour16-17 From Depression To _______ [mode of Dum Spiro Spero], and Tour16-17 From Depression To _______ [mode of ].

Vestige of Scratches, their compilation album, was released on January 2, 2018. "Ningen wo Kaburu," Dir en Grey's twenty-ninth single, was released on April 25, 2018. On September 26, 2018, Dir en Grey released their tenth studio album, The Insulated World. The band also announced a European tour called "Wearing Human Skin" that will begin in October 2018. This tour was followed by a North American tour in December 2019 and a second European tour in January and February 2020.

"The World of Mercy," their 30th single, was released on September 18, 2019. On August 3rd, 2020, their 31st single, "Ochita Koto no Aru Sora," was released digitally. They released their 32nd single, "Oboro," on April 28th, 2021. Phalaris, their 11th studio album, was released in June.
